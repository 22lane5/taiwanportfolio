Spikeball

My experience playing spikeball in Taiwan

Before even arriving in Taiwan I knew I wanted to find ways to get involved with local sports groups as I wanted to keep active and connect with local people over something I enjoyed. One of my mutual friends was Taiwanese and was apart of the spikeball scene in Taipei so she helped connect me with some people.

After landing in Taipei at 6AM on Sunday I was determined to stay awake till night time and do my best to avoid jet lag. After settling into my airbnb I messaged around to figure out if there was any pickup spikeball that day. Luckily there was later that afternoon. So afer lunch I hopped on the bus and 20 minutes later I was playing spikeball in a random Taipei park. That day, most of the people there were also foreigners. Many were taiwanese americans who were in Taiwan to teach english, some for a few years and others close to twenty. I played from two until close to six after which some of us went and ate dinner at a local Taiwanese restaurant. We ended up hanging out till around nine and by the time I got to bed it was around ten and after a full night's sleep jet lag was avoided.

During the middle of the week I decided to go to a session with a more advanced group of players. This time, my communte involved walking half a mile to the subway station, riding the subway for 25 minutes, and then walking around a mile through a night market followed by a somewhat sketchy looking neighborhood. Upon showing up I could tell this was more advanced as players were wearing jerseys and cleats to play a recreational backyard sport. All jokes aside these players were really good and some were "premeire level", which in the niche world of competitive spikeball basically means pro level. At 10pm, after three hours of playing it was time to start my trek home after taking several hits to my spikeball ego and learning I really wasn't all that good. As I was about to head out the group graciously offered to give me a ride to the subway station and invited me to get food with them first. So after weaving around on the back of a moped at questionable speeds (I had a helmet) we sat down to eat at a late night taiwanese breakfast shop which sold classics like soy milk and egg pancakes. What I forgot to mention was unlike Sunday which was mostly foreigners, today's crowd was all Taiwanese people most of whom knew little to know english.
